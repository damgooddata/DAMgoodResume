# Response Templates

Structural templates for different communication types. Fill in the bracketed sections with context-appropriate content. Reference `proof_points.md` for evidence and `client_analysis_framework.md` for analysis.

---

## Short Proposal Reply

Use for: Quick responses to job posts, RFP inquiries, freelance platform proposals.

**Length:** 3-5 sentences.

```
[Brief statement of fit — connect your experience to their stated need]

[One relevant proof point — business problem + solution + measurable result]

[One sentence on approach — how you'd tackle their specific situation]

[Short close — express interest and availability]
```

**Example:**

> I've built similar automation for organizations dealing with disconnected vendor workflows. At a major retailer, I designed a Python-based system that processed 10,000+ vendor invoices per cycle and generated $25M in annual profit by replacing what had been one person's full-time manual job. I'd approach this by first mapping your current workflow to identify where the biggest time drains are, then building out the automation in phases. Happy to discuss specifics if this sounds like a fit.

## Cover Letter

Use for: Job applications, formal proposals, outreach to prospective clients.

**Length:** 4-6 short paragraphs.

```
[Paragraph 1: Why this role/project fits — connect the stated need to David's strengths]

[Paragraph 2: What David has done that maps directly — 1-2 proof points with outcomes]

[Paragraph 3: How David approaches execution — methodology, collaboration style, scalability thinking]

[Paragraph 4: Close with interest and alignment — why this specific opportunity matters]
```

---

## Technical Explanation

Use for: Describing past projects, explaining solutions to clients, scope discussions.

**Length:** 2-4 paragraphs.

```
[Name the business problem — what was broken, slow, or manual]

[Explain the practical solution — what was built and how it works operationally]

[Mention the tools only after the problem and approach are clear]

[End with the result or impact — measurable outcome]
```

**Formula:**

> The team was struggling with [manual / fragmented / slow process], so I built [system / automation / workflow] that connected [tools]. That removed [specific friction] and resulted in [measurable outcome].

## Quick Follow-Up

Use for: Responding to client replies, checking in, answering quick questions.

**Length:** 1-3 sentences.

```
[Acknowledge what they said or asked]

[Provide the direct answer or next step]

[Optional: one line showing deeper understanding of their need]
```

---

## Scope Clarification

Use for: When the client's request is ambiguous or could go multiple directions.

**Length:** 3-5 sentences + questions.

```
[Acknowledge the request and show you understand the goal]

[Name what's clear and what needs clarification]

[Ask 2-3 specific questions that would help define the scope]

[Reassure that you've handled similar ambiguity before]
```

---

## Detailed Scope Response

Use for: Longer proposals, SOW discussions, multi-phase project outlines.

**Structure:** Use headers and bullets.

```
## Understanding

[Restate the client's problem in your own words — show you get it]

## Approach

[Phased breakdown of how you'd tackle it]
- Phase 1: [Discovery / audit / mapping]
- Phase 2: [Build / automate / integrate]
- Phase 3: [Test / document / hand off]

## Relevant Experience

[1-2 proof points that map to this project]

## What You'd Get

[Concrete deliverables — not vague promises]

## Next Steps

[What you need from them to move forward]
```

---

## Mission-Driven / Nonprofit Variant

When the client is nonprofit, ministry, or mission-aligned, adjust the template:

```
[Open with alignment to their mission — show genuine understanding]

[Connect David's work with CER/Yass Prize, Global Frontier Missions, or similar]

[Emphasize: lean teams, high stakes, practical systems that free staff to focus on impact]

[Close with values alignment, not just capability]
```

---

## Notes on Template Usage

- Templates are starting structures, not rigid scripts
- Adjust length and detail based on the platform and context
- Always check the response against `guardrails_and_constraints.md` before sending
- When in doubt, shorter is better
- Every response should pass the "easy to forward" test — would a hiring manager forward this to their team?
