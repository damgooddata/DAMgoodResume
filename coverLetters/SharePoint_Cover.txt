4. What are the most insightful tips you have found using SharePoint for security/access level of folders for internal and external users?

The single most important thing: never manage permissions manually. The moment you start hand-assigning folder access through the SharePoint UI, you're on borrowed time before someone gets access to something they shouldn't. It's not a matter of if, it's when.

Use security groups in Entra ID (Azure AD) and assign permissions at the group level, not the individual level. When someone joins or leaves, you update the group and the permissions cascade automatically. If you layer an API-driven process on top of that, you get a system that is faster, more secure, and extremely accurate compared to anything a person could maintain by clicking through the UI.

A few other things I've learned:
- Break inheritance intentionally and document it. SharePoint permission inheritance is powerful, but once you break it at a subfolder level, it's easy to lose track of who has access to what. The API lets you audit this programmatically.
- For external users, use SharePoint sharing links with expiration dates rather than adding them as full site members. It keeps your tenant cleaner and gives you more control.
- Build an access audit into your automation. Even with a well-designed permission model, run a periodic script that reports who has access to what. It catches drift before it becomes a problem.
